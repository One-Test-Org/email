He discovered computers in the mid 80s where as a kid he built databases for Video stores. He run his father's caretakers monthly figures for his blocks of apartments where he grew up. He even coded sprites for his mother's photo-shop to use in video editing titles.

After the army, he learned the Adobe software and started his career as a graphic designer. It was't until late 2009 that his late brother needed a website for his business, and he design it. That was the start of his career as a web designer. A few years later he started working for the Department of Work and Pensions as a Service Designer. He now is a Lead Interaction designer working as a contractor for the Home Office.
